Research Rules

Date-keyed macro heuristics extracted from published Macro Lensing entries for reuse in research notebooks, engines, and later reviews.

2026-06-13

Fed Wildcard Meets Dollar Growth Scare

  • Do not treat lower oil as automatically bullish. Separate geopolitical relief from demand destruction.
  • A true risk extension needs dollar weakness, China/EM confirmation, metals stabilization, and technology participation.
  • When rate-sensitive sectors rally before a Fed event, read them as policy expectations first and proof second.
  • Financials can be bullish long-term if they make quiet highs and then pull back; euphoric chasing can turn the same chart bearish later.
  • Do not buy damaged parabolic semiconductor structures just because a three-wave support zone exists; a broken parabola can retrace far beyond a normal fourth wave.
  • Silver and miners are less reliable if gold has not reached its own reset area; metals need alignment.
  • Volatility products require defined entries and close stops. Random volatility buying is not the same as buying a structured volatility turn.
  • In connector-wave markets, the next level is less important than whether the move subdivides cleanly into a tradable structure.

2026-06-11

Headline Whipsaw Traps The Middle Trade

  • Do not trade the middle of an unfinished corrective structure. Wait for a level where the stop and invalidation are visible.
  • A squeeze can invalidate a stale support zone without creating a new bullish regime. Rebuild the map after the next turn.
  • For inverse or bearish exposure, require a tight stop and a clean three-wave pullback or higher-low structure; otherwise the trade is just shorting emotion.
  • When policy headlines drive price, treat the first move as liquidity information rather than truth. The second reaction matters more.
  • Semiconductors, financials, volatility, dollar, and yen should be read together. A single bullish channel is not enough to confirm broad risk-on.
  • In speculative IPO or event weeks, expect capital to support excitement first and ask price-discovery questions later.

2026-06-10

Parabolic Risk Enters A Waterfall Test

  • Treat major support areas as tests, not conclusions. A level only gains authority after reaction, divergence, and reclaim behavior confirm it.
  • When leadership and volatility disagree with price support, trust the cross-asset pressure first and wait for the chart to repair.
  • Do not let a weak dollar alone define the risk regime. If equities fail to rally with dollar relief, internal stress is more important than the headline FX input.
  • Separate inflation rotation from demand destruction in energy and materials. The same commodity move can carry opposite portfolio messages depending on breadth, rates, and dollar behavior.
  • For parabolic leaders, a first support tag after a broken structure is a review point, not a value signal.
  • Use volatility thresholds as position-size governors. Rising volatility changes the acceptable error band even when the direction call is unchanged.

2026-06-09

China Growth Shock Reprices The Risk Tape

  • Do not score lower oil as bullish unless copper, China, breadth, and cyclicals are also stabilizing.
  • When China and EM correct a higher-degree cycle, reduce confidence in broad risk expansion until the downtrend breaks.
  • Require volatility contraction and dollar weakness before upgrading relief rallies into regime repair.
  • Parabola breaks in high-beta leadership should discount the first normal support zone unless shorter-cycle divergence repairs.
  • If leadership narrows into semiconductors alone, treat the market as fragile even if headline indexes bounce.
  • Large daily support zones need momentum repair; a fast, no-divergence drop into support can pierce the zone before any real low forms.
  • Late-cycle rallies into overhead supply are tactical tests, not automatic permission to expand risk.

2026-06-08

Dollar, Yen, And Volatility Put Risk On Defense

  • When VIX is above its shelf and dollar strength is threatening, lower the confidence score for high-beta risk exposure.
  • When a support area is reached but reaction is weak, treat that as negative information rather than a reason to widen risk.
  • Second-attempt risk exposures should be cleaner than missed original levels. A lower level is only useful if the macro regime still allows risk.
  • Industrial, financial, housing, and materials breakdowns should increase regime-risk weight faster than isolated mega-cap weakness.
  • A dollar-up / yen-down mix deserves a special stress flag because it can convert normal equity correction into liquidity-event behavior.

2026-06-06

Risk Curve Enters A Defensive Transition Test

  • Dollar strength should be scored as a tightening impulse when it pressures metals, crypto, commodities, and emerging markets at the same time.
  • Leadership failure in semis, AI, and mega-cap tech can precede the headline index breakdown and should carry early-warning weight.
  • Support zones deserve lower confidence when price reaches them through forced liquidation rather than controlled pullback.
  • Volatility reclaim after a failed breakdown is a hedging-demand signal, not background noise.
  • Strategic resource conviction should not override tactical liquidity stress when the dollar and rates are both pressuring risk.

2026-06-04

Rate Relief Broadens The Risk Advance

  • Breadth expansion after softer labor data should raise risk-on confidence only if pullbacks remain controlled.
  • A gap below support is not equivalent to a normal support test; score it as liquidity stress.
  • Consumer discretionary and financial breakouts should be treated as macro confirmation channels, not just sector-specific chart events.
  • Bitcoin dominance rising can support flagship crypto while still warning that speculative liquidity is narrowing.
  • Long-term resource conviction should wait for tactical confirmation when the dollar and gold/silver ratio remain unresolved.
  • When the market fears missing upside, shallow high-beta levels need stricter confirmation because crowding risk rises.

2026-06-03

Commodity Signals Split The Macro Tape

  • Treat cross-asset agreement as stronger evidence than any single index pattern.
  • When relief rallies arrive, require volatility, dollar, breadth, and leadership to confirm before upgrading regime posture.
  • Dollar strength should be scored as a tightening impulse when it pressures commodities, crypto, emerging markets, and high-beta equities together.
  • A volatility shelf that refuses to break lower should keep rally confidence capped until price confirms otherwise.
  • Technology leadership should be tested against breadth; narrow leadership is less durable when cyclicals and small caps lag.
  • Commodity strength should be separated into inflation impulse, demand strength, and liquidity hedge before assigning regime meaning.
  • Mixed tapes should be traded as confirmation problems: wait for one side of the capital-flow stack to resolve.

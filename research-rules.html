Research Rules

Date-keyed macro heuristics extracted from published Macro Lensing entries for reuse in research notebooks, engines, and later reviews.

2026-06-23

Dollar Breakout Tests The Last Tech Bid

  • A narrow chip-led rally is not a broad market signal until breadth confirms outside semiconductors and memory.
  • Dollar strength plus yen stress is a stronger warning than either signal alone because it points to global funding pressure.
  • Volatility analysis improves when tied to a trigger level. Treat the breakout as confirmation, not as background noise.
  • Do not upgrade risk because oil is falling until the move is classified as supply relief, demand destruction, or late-cycle positioning flush.
  • Obvious commodity support levels require cross-market confirmation. Gold , silver , and ratio signals should agree before the setup is trusted.
  • Small-cap weakness matters more when rates and volatility are rising together.
  • Middle zones are not neutral; they are where poor timing converts correct macro reads into bad trades.

2026-06-22

Dollar And Semis Put Risk On Trial

  • A crowded leadership sleeve can support indexes longer than expected, but it should lower confidence in broad risk until breadth confirms.
  • Dollar support plus rising rates should downgrade automatic dip-buying in crypto , metals, EM, commodities , software, small caps , and high beta.
  • Lower oil is not automatically risk-on. Separate demand destruction, supply relief, positioning flushes, and physical-market tightness before upgrading liquidity.
  • Volatility warnings become more useful when tied to a concrete trigger level. Treat the trigger as regime confirmation, not just decoration.
  • EM strength must be decomposed. If the strength is mostly memory, Taiwan, Korea, and chip-linked exposure, it is not clean global breadth.
  • Energy entries should respect both chart structure and positioning. A crowded short base can create violent squeezes even before the cleanest low appears.
  • When price sits in the middle of the structure, patience is a position. The market has not paid enough for certainty.

2026-06-20

Dollar Pressure Tests The AI Relief Trade

  • Lower oil is not automatically risk-on. Classify the move as supply repair, demand destruction, or temporary paper relief before upgrading equities.
  • A strong dollar after policy tightening should lower confidence in EM, crypto , commodities , metals, and high-beta setups until dollar failure is confirmed.
  • Headline index strength needs breadth validation when semiconductors are the main support beam.
  • AI infrastructure is a different trade from AI multiple expansion. Power, grid, storage, and industrial execution can become healthier expressions than crowded chips.
  • Commodity baskets can reveal inflation pressure before oil headlines fully reverse. Treat commodity support as a warning against simple disinflation narratives.
  • Copper is a regime validator for industrial cyclicality and AI buildout. Copper failure weakens the growth case even if indexes hold.
  • Do not force trades in middle zones. A no-action zone is information: the market has not paid enough for the risk.

2026-06-17

Fed Regime Shift Tightens The Risk Tape

  • When policy language changes, trade the reaction function, not the headline. A fast central bank changes the value of every support zone.
  • Require dollar confirmation before trusting risk-on setups. Dollar strength can invalidate bullish reads across metals, crypto, growth, and emerging markets at once.
  • Separate support from structure. A support area reached in clean corrective form is different from a straight-line liquidation into the same area.
  • Use VIX as a regime switch, not just a sentiment gauge. A move above the high-teens trigger changes the odds of forced selling.
  • Read energy through two lenses: inflation persistence if support holds, demand destruction if support fails.
  • For semiconductors, distinguish reset from distribution. Deep corrective damage that later rebuilds can be bullish; weak sideways failure near highs is not.

2026-06-16

Fed, Dollar, And Oil Put Risk On Trial

  • Treat lower oil as ambiguous until demand, supply, and sector breadth tell you whether it is relief or deflation.
  • Do not upgrade a risk rally without dollar confirmation. Dollar strength can invalidate bullish reads across multiple assets at once.
  • Require breadth to confirm policy relief. A few leaders can squeeze while the broader capital-flow stack remains defensive.
  • Use copper as the growth/reflection validator. Copper failure weakens the case for a healthy cyclical expansion.
  • For overextended leadership sectors, distinguish a reset from distribution. A sharp corrective flush can be constructive; sideways failure near highs is more dangerous.
  • A support zone or support area is not a thesis by itself. The path into it matters: clean subdivisions support a reaction; straight-line liquidation warns of deeper stress.

2026-06-15

Dollar And Yen Test The Relief Pump

  • Treat geopolitical headlines as secondary until dollar, yen, rates, and volatility confirm them.
  • Do not call a three-wave rally durable until it either makes a credible fifth wave or erases bearish divergence.
  • Separate lower crude from better growth: oil down from demand destruction is not the same thing as oil down from benign supply relief.
  • Use the gold/silver ratio as the timing filter for metals; silver is most attractive after ratio pressure exhausts, not while it is accelerating.
  • When a support zone is late in a grander cycle, size the idea as a trade first and a thesis second.
  • Let policy windows finish before pretending the regime is settled; BOJ and Fed events can reverse the read in a single session.

2026-06-14

Headline Pump Meets The Dollar Test

  • Do not upgrade a headline-driven futures squeeze unless dollar, volatility, breadth, and cash-session follow-through confirm it.
  • Oil relief is not the same as physical normalization; shipping, insurance, security, and policy timing all matter.
  • The Fed window overrides the headline window: easier conditions can extend risk, while hawkish inflation language can reverse the pump.
  • When the dollar back-tests a major range, treat that level as the liquidity hinge for equities, metals, crypto, and emerging risk.
  • Yen risk is nonlinear. Dollar strength plus orderly yen weakness is correction fuel; dollar strength plus yen-policy shock is deleveraging fuel.
  • Parabolic semiconductor leadership needs an exit framework before the break, not after the drawdown becomes intolerable.
  • Copper should be scored as the industrial-growth verdict: breakout confirms growth impulse, failure confirms demand-destruction risk.
  • Single-name chart reviews are breadth samples first and candidate setups second; use them to judge whether risk appetite is broadening or only clustering in crowded winners.

2026-06-13

Fed Wildcard Meets Dollar Growth Scare

  • Do not treat lower oil as automatically bullish. Separate geopolitical relief from demand destruction.
  • A true risk extension needs dollar weakness, China/EM confirmation, metals stabilization, and technology participation.
  • When rate-sensitive sectors rally before a Fed event, read them as policy expectations first and proof second.
  • Financials can be bullish long-term if they make quiet highs and then pull back; euphoric chasing can turn the same chart bearish later.
  • Do not buy damaged parabolic semiconductor structures just because a three-wave support zone exists; a broken parabola can retrace far beyond a normal fourth wave.
  • Silver and miners are less reliable if gold has not reached its own reset area; metals need alignment.
  • Volatility products require defined entries and close stops. Random volatility buying is not the same as buying a structured volatility turn.
  • In connector-wave markets, the next level is less important than whether the move subdivides cleanly into a tradable structure.

2026-06-11

Headline Whipsaw Traps The Middle Trade

  • Do not trade the middle of an unfinished corrective structure. Wait for a level where the stop and invalidation are visible.
  • A squeeze can invalidate a stale support zone without creating a new bullish regime. Rebuild the map after the next turn.
  • For inverse or bearish exposure, require a tight stop and a clean three-wave pullback or higher-low structure; otherwise the trade is just shorting emotion.
  • When policy headlines drive price, treat the first move as liquidity information rather than truth. The second reaction matters more.
  • Semiconductors, financials, volatility, dollar, and yen should be read together. A single bullish channel is not enough to confirm broad risk-on.
  • In speculative IPO or event weeks, expect capital to support excitement first and ask price-discovery questions later.

2026-06-10

Parabolic Risk Enters A Waterfall Test

  • Treat major support areas as tests, not conclusions. A level only gains authority after reaction, divergence, and reclaim behavior confirm it.
  • When leadership and volatility disagree with price support, trust the cross-asset pressure first and wait for the chart to repair.
  • Do not let a weak dollar alone define the risk regime. If equities fail to rally with dollar relief, internal stress is more important than the headline FX input.
  • Separate inflation rotation from demand destruction in energy and materials. The same commodity move can carry opposite portfolio messages depending on breadth, rates, and dollar behavior.
  • For parabolic leaders, a first support tag after a broken structure is a review point, not a value signal.
  • Use volatility thresholds as position-size governors. Rising volatility changes the acceptable error band even when the direction call is unchanged.

2026-06-09

China Growth Shock Reprices The Risk Tape

  • Do not score lower oil as bullish unless copper, China, breadth, and cyclicals are also stabilizing.
  • When China and EM correct a higher-degree cycle, reduce confidence in broad risk expansion until the downtrend breaks.
  • Require volatility contraction and dollar weakness before upgrading relief rallies into regime repair.
  • Parabola breaks in high-beta leadership should discount the first normal support zone unless shorter-cycle divergence repairs.
  • If leadership narrows into semiconductors alone, treat the market as fragile even if headline indexes bounce.
  • Large daily support zones need momentum repair; a fast, no-divergence drop into support can pierce the zone before any real low forms.
  • Late-cycle rallies into overhead supply are tactical tests, not automatic permission to expand risk.

2026-06-08

Dollar, Yen, And Volatility Put Risk On Defense

  • When VIX is above its shelf and dollar strength is threatening, lower the confidence score for high-beta risk exposure.
  • When a support area is reached but reaction is weak, treat that as negative information rather than a reason to widen risk.
  • Second-attempt risk exposures should be cleaner than missed original levels. A lower level is only useful if the macro regime still allows risk.
  • Industrial, financial, housing, and materials breakdowns should increase regime-risk weight faster than isolated mega-cap weakness.
  • A dollar-up / yen-down mix deserves a special stress flag because it can convert normal equity correction into liquidity-event behavior.

2026-06-06

Risk Curve Enters A Defensive Transition Test

  • Dollar strength should be scored as a tightening impulse when it pressures metals, crypto, commodities, and emerging markets at the same time.
  • Leadership failure in semis, AI, and mega-cap tech can precede the headline index breakdown and should carry early-warning weight.
  • Support zones deserve lower confidence when price reaches them through forced liquidation rather than controlled pullback.
  • Volatility reclaim after a failed breakdown is a hedging-demand signal, not background noise.
  • Strategic resource conviction should not override tactical liquidity stress when the dollar and rates are both pressuring risk.
